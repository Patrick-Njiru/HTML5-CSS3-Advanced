  2. Use cases for various elements in HTML5 for accessibility purposes

    strong
    to convey importance, seriousness or urgency
    b
    no importance is being conveyed
    i
    to show a technical term, transliterations, a thought, a taxonomic designation, a idiomatic phrase from another language ...
    em
    for stress emphasis

  3. Logical vs Physical Tag

    logical - have a specific purpose. examples : strong, header

    physical - don't really have a special purpose. examples : b, div

  4. Creating (Symbols and) Spaces Between Elements or Words

    We can use HTML Entities like nonbreak space and medium space.. You can use them as many times as you would like to get the amount of space you need.
    example: I Am &MediumSpace;&MediumSpace; Patrick   ≡ I Am    Patrick

    We can also use the pre element which follows the rule - what you see is what you get.
    example(look at source code): 

                            I       Am      Patrick
                        Njiru

    with ampersand(&) you can create very many symbols like λ, ∞, γ, <, >, β, α, © ...

  6. iframes

    Alows embedding of websites or parts of a website into a your website. Provides interactivity from external websites without leaving your website.
    It can be used to display maps, YouTube videos etc. to embed YouTube videos, one can just click the share option under the video and click the embed option.
    Some websites like Google don't allow to be embedded due to security reasons.
